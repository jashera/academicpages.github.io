--- layout: archive title: "Boo!" permalink: /test.html author_profile: true --- There are limited treatments for individuals who experience a loss of visual function as a result of a stroke. Yet, it is reported approximately 60% of stroke survivors will have a permanent visual impairment. Recent research indicates that the visual system can learn new information, and improvements in perception are observed as a result of training. Perceptual training, therefore, may be a potential tool for visual rehabilitation. However, the rehabilitation of the visual areas through perceptual learning is a controversial topic, as it is still not yet fully understood where learning is taking place, and what, exactly, is being learned.
